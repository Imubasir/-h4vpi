About Us

Heritage for Voluntary Partnership Initiatives (H4VPI) is a voluntary non-profit organization established with the sole aim of creating opportunities for the empowerment of the vulnerable in society and providing educational support to deprived children in rural communities in Northern Ghana.

It largely depends on support in the form of cash and kind, from international volunteers, groups and individuals donations, who are touched by the need to contribute to empower vulnerable women and meeting the educational needs of deprived children in rural Ghana. It focuses primarily on rural communities deprived of basic social amenities. Whiles receiving this support, the women and children are also empowered to learn about how to protect the environment in which they live. This, they do by embarking on tree planting in their communities.

Basic Education Foundation Project (BEFoP)

H4VPI is a voluntary organization that focuses on the provision of educational needs to basic schools in northern Ghana. Its BEFoP is committed to supporting basic schools through voluntary support in kind and in cash, to among others, undertake the following;

  • donating teaching and learning materials
  • collaborating for voluntary teaching and mentoring
  • internship support activities
  • soliciting assistance to provide school equipment
  • developing proposals to fund the construction of school infrastructure

Marginalized Women Empowerment Project (MWEP)

MWEP is a project initiative under H4VPI that undertakes various livelihood development projects to promote empowerment of the vulnerable. The project is a collaboration between H4VPI and the interested volunteers, who raise support and recommend volunteers, students and family, who wish to travel to Ghana to support the work of H4VPI with focus on the following project areas

  • Entrepreneurial training and capacity building for vulnerable women groups
  • Providing livelihood schemes for vulnerable women groups
  • Internship support activitie
  • Supporting Self-Help women projects
  • Cross cultural and transformation learning
